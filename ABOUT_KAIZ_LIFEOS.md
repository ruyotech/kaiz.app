Most apps collect intentions. **Kaiz produces outcomes.**

- Pick a goal, not 200 wishes

- Execute with focus, not scattered effort

## The Life Wheel That Makes "Balance" Real

Balance shouldn't be a mood. It should be visible.

Kaiz tracks every action against the **9 Life Wheel dimensions** and shows you:

- what you're investing in (and what you're neglecting)
- how your life is trending over weeks/months
- when your "Career spike" is silently killing "Family" or "Health"

**Example:**
You finish a sprint with 90% Career tasks and zero Relationships. Kaiz flags it and suggests the smallest recovery move:
*"Add one Q2 Family task next sprint: 'Plan Saturday outing (2 pts)'."*

Balance becomes a system, not a speech.

## Weekly Sprints That Respect Physics (Capacity + Guardrails)

Kaiz makes you choose what fits — and protects you from overcommit.

Every week you run a real Sprint:

- planned points vs completed points
- velocity trend (your real capacity)
- churn (mid-week additions)
- carryover debt (unfinished work that keeps haunting you)

**Example:**
Your average velocity is 28 points. Sunday planning tries to commit 55. Kaiz doesn't "recommend"—it guards:
*"Overcommit risk: +27 points. Pick what ships."*

**This single feature ends the productivity lie: "I'll just do more."**

## Prioritization That Actually Changes Decisions (Eisenhower Built-In)

Kaiz turns your backlog into a decision engine.

You can instantly view everything in the **Eisenhower Matrix**:

- **Q1:** urgent crises
- **Q2:** growth zone (where your future is built)
- **Q3:** noise
- **Q4:** waste

**The Kaiz move:** it watches your Q2.

If you keep living in Q1 firefighting, Kaiz nudges you to reclaim Q2 with one strategic task.

That's how your life stops being reactive.

## Calendar as the Command Center (Past • Current • Future Sprints)

The calendar isn't just dates — it's where your life becomes operational.

From the calendar you can:

- switch Past / Current / Future sprints in one tap
- see tasks directly on days with status + Eisenhower + life dimension

- overlay your Google/Outlook calendar read-only to reflect reality

**Examples:**

- "This week is crushed by meetings" — Kaiz sees your blocked time and warns when sprint scope doesn't fit.

**Kaiz turns time into a schedule, not a hope.**

## Finance Hub That Never Loses History (Provider-Independent Continuity)

This is the "adulting" feature most apps mess up.

Kaiz tracks bills by **what they are**, not who the vendor is:

- internet stays "Internet" even if you switch Comcast → AT&T → Spectrum
- electricity history stays continuous even if providers change
- every bill keeps attachments tied to the record for audit/taxes

**Example:**
Two years later, you can pull:
- every HOA payment
- every insurance bill
- electricity trends by month
- before/after switching providers

**Your history doesn't reset when life changes. Kaiz is built for continuity.**
